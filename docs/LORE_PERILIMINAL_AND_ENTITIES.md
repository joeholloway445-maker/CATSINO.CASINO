# Periliminal: The Gauntlet's Psychology

## The Gauntlet Mechanism: Tailored Horror

Periliminal is not a place; it's a *personal gauntlet* generated from your Hope profile. It's the six layers *inverted and weaponized against you specifically*.

### How Periliminal Reads Your Profile

The gauntlet scans your Hope profile's axes:

- **Aggression**: How much you *initiate* conflict vs. avoid it.
- **Caution**: How risk-averse you are.
- **Curiosity**: How much you're drawn to mystery and the unknown.
- **Greed**: How acquisitive and resource-focused you are.
- **Fear**: Your baseline anxiety and aversion to danger.
- **Lust**: Sexual and sensual desire, or thirst for power.
- **Boredom**: How quickly you lose interest in stasis or repetition.
- **Anxiety**: Your resistance to psychological pressure and moral ambiguity.

For each axis, Periliminal constructs a *trap*.

---

## The Gauntlet's Traps: Mapping Hope to Horror

### Trap for High-Aggression Players: **The Arena of Endless Combat**

**Design**: You're placed in an arena with *increasingly* difficult entities. Each victory is rewarded with progression, but there's no *exit*—there's always a stronger opponent waiting. The gauntlet is designed to make you *chase* the next fight.

**The Twist**: The entities aren't actually getting stronger; you're getting *weaker*. The gauntlet is slowing your movements frame by frame, reducing your health regeneration, stacking status effects. You *feel* more powerful because you're winning, but you're actually deteriorating.

**The Choice**: Keep fighting (and die exhausted), or *stop* (and face the shame of giving up). The blessing door appears at the bottom of a seemingly unending floor of enemies. To reach it, you must stop fighting and *walk past* the entities, accepting their attacks.

**Psychological Insight**: The aggressive player is trapped by their own nature. What saves them is accepting *passivity* and vulnerability.

---

### Trap for High-Caution Players: **The Hall of Falling Certainties**

**Design**: You're in a hallway where every surface *seems* stable, but one step reveals that the ground beneath you isn't real. You're in Liminal's logic—nothing is solid. Every forward movement is a gamble. Entities lurk at the ends of hallways, but going back is equally dangerous.

**The Twist**: The entities aren't actually there; they're hallucinations born of your anxiety. The *real* danger is that your caution is paralyzing you. You could move forward—there's an exit—but you're frozen because you can't *guarantee* safety.

**The Choice**: Move forward into uncertainty, or stay paralyzed. The blessing door requires you to take a *leap of faith* through a void that *looks* fatal but isn't.

**Psychological Insight**: The cautious player is trapped by the illusion of control. What saves them is trusting.

---

### Trap for High-Curiosity Players: **The Library of Forbidden Answers**

**Design**: You're in an infinite library. Every book offers answers—true answers—to your deepest questions. What are the layers? Why do they exist? Who controls them? But each book you read *costs* something (a memory, a relationship, a year of your subjective lifespan).

**The Twist**: The answers are real and useful, but the *cost is real*. You can solve all the puzzles of the game by reading enough books, but you'll become something hollow and disconnected. The knowledge is poisonous.

**The Choice**: Resist the temptation to read, or accept the knowledge and its cost. The blessing door is hidden in a book that offers no answers—just a blank page and the option to leave.

**Psychological Insight**: The curious player is trapped by their drive for understanding. What saves them is accepting *mystery* and *not knowing*.

---

### Trap for High-Greed Players: **The Infinite Vault**

**Design**: You're in a vault filled with *infinite* resources—currency, entities, rare items, titles. You can take as much as you want, but the deeper you go, the more *dangerous* it becomes. Entities guard deeper sections. The air becomes toxic. Your inventory weight limit forces you to choose what to take.

**The Twist**: The vault is *actually* finite. At the very bottom, there's nothing but a mirror. All the resources you collected are *illusions* that vanish if you try to leave the gauntlet with them. You've been accumulating nothing.

**The Choice**: Collect what you can and try to escape with it (you'll fail), or accept that the resources are meaningless and simply *walk to the exit*.

**Psychological Insight**: The greedy player is trapped by scarcity mindset. What saves them is accepting abundance through *letting go*.

---

### Trap for High-Fear Players: **The Personified Terror**

**Design**: The gauntlet manifests as a *entity made entirely from your fears*. It's not a specific form—it shifts. If you fear intimacy, it appears as a seductive figure that promises connection then betrayal. If you fear failure, it appears as every critical voice in your life, telling you you're worthless. If you fear death, it appears as a version of yourself, *already dead*, asking why you're still alive.

**The Twist**: The entity is incorporeal. It can't actually hurt you. It's pure psychology. The only way to "defeat" it is to *face it* without running, hear what it has to say, and accept it as part of yourself.

**The Choice**: Fight it (impossible—you can't damage what's intangible), flee it (it follows you forever), or *accept it* and integrate it. The blessing door is inside the entity. You walk through its chest.

**Psychological Insight**: The fearful player is trapped by avoidance. What saves them is *radical acceptance*.

---

### Trap for High-Lust Players: **The Halls of Desire**

**Design**: You encounter every person, every entity, every *thing* that you're attracted to. They offer themselves to you, no consequences, no judgment. You can indulge every impulse. Entities bond instantly to you. NPCs fall into your arms. The world is designed entirely around your desire.

**The Twist**: As you indulge, you become increasingly *hollow*. Your sense of self dissolves into appetite. At the end, there's nothing left of you—just a wanting-void.

**The Choice**: Resist temptation (painful and lonely), or indulge and lose yourself. The blessing door requires you to turn *away* from everything you desire and walk alone into the dark.

**Psychological Insight**: The lustful player is trapped by desire-chasing. What saves them is *discipline* and *self-definition*.

---

### Trap for High-Boredom Players: **The Eternal Waiting Room**

**Design**: You're in a waiting room. The walls are blank. Nothing happens. Ever. There's no combat, no interaction, no stimulation. Time passes. Days, weeks. Or does it? You can't tell how long you've been here. You could leave at any time, but leaving feels like quitting.

**The Twist**: Leaving *is* the correct choice. There's no exit because *there's nothing to overcome*. The trap isn't a challenge; it's *acceptance of stasis*.

**The Choice**: Stay waiting (die of boredom/despair), or accept that not every moment is an adventure and walk out through the obvious door.

**Psychological Insight**: The bored player is trapped by constant stimulation-seeking. What saves them is finding *meaning* in simple existence.

---

### Trap for High-Anxiety Players: **The Moral Gauntlet**

**Design**: You face a series of *moral dilemmas* with no clear answers. Do you sacrifice an NPC to save a faction? Do you betray allies to progress? Do you compromise your values for power? Each choice locks you into a consequence. There are no good options.

**The Twist**: The "correct" answer is that *there is no correct answer*. Moral ambiguity is the gauntlet's core. You're being tested not for righteousness but for *willingness to act despite uncertainty*.

**The Choice**: Make decisions despite anxiety, or be paralyzed by indecision. The blessing door requires you to *commit* to a choice you're not sure about and live with it.

**Psychological Insight**: The anxious player is trapped by perfectionism. What saves them is accepting *moral imperfection*.

## Blessing Door Access

The **blessing door** only appears after you've reached a certain depth in the gauntlet that's *personalized to your difficulty*. Your Hope profile's overall risk/reward-seeking determines the minimum depth required:

- **Aggressive players**: Minimum depth 15 floors (you exhaust yourself)
- **Cautious players**: Minimum depth 12 floors (you overcome your fear)
- **Curious players**: Minimum depth 14 floors (you resist knowledge)
- **Greedy players**: Minimum depth 13 floors (you let go)
- **Fearful players**: Minimum depth 16 floors (you integrate terror)
- **Lustful players**: Minimum depth 14 floors (you achieve discipline)
- **Bored players**: Minimum depth 8 floors (less challenge needed—already accepting monotony)
- **Anxious players**: Minimum depth 17 floors (moral weight accumulates)

The **Periliminal difficulty** curve multiplies this:
- Cruel/reckless players face *deeper* gaunts (up to +7 floors).
- Balanced players face *standard* depths.
- Compassionate players face *shallower* gaunts (can exit earlier).

Once you reach your minimum depth, the blessing door manifests. Exiting through it grants:
- A permanent buff (title + stat bonus specific to your trap type)
- A guaranteed rare entity capture
- Temporary invulnerability (the other layers can't hurt you for ~1 hour after blessing door exit)
- An achievement viewable by other players (cosmetic reputation)

## Entities: What They Represent

Entities aren't just mechanical companions. Each of the 600 authored entities in the dex is a *manifestation* of a psychological or physical archetype. They're divided into six categories across 270 creature lines:

### **Energy Entities: Boundless Force**

These are entities that represent *raw potential* and *intensity*. They're manifestations of passion, drive, and power without direction. They're the most "heroic" on first glance—beautiful, powerful, inspiring.

**Thematic**: They want to *act*. They're impatient with plans; they want to engage immediately. Bonding with them teaches you about *agency* and *momentum*.

**Psychological Mirror**: Energy entities represent your own drive to accomplish things. Players who bond with many Energy entities are often achievement-oriented and action-driven.

**Periliminal Correlation**: Energy entities appear frequently to aggressive/active players, because they *amplify* those drives.

---

### **Entropy Entities: Inevitable Decay**

These are manifestations of *mortality, change, and dissolution*. They represent the *ending* of things—relationships, worlds, selves. They're beautiful in their resignation.

**Thematic**: They accept change and loss. They're wise in a melancholic way. Bonding with them teaches you about *acceptance* and *letting go*.

**Psychological Mirror**: Entropy entities represent your shadow side—the part of you that *wants* things to end, that finds relief in finality. They're often dark-colored and slow-moving.

**Periliminal Correlation**: Entropy entities appear to anxious/grieving players. They offer comfort through acceptance of loss.

---

### **Gravity Entities: Weight and Consequence**

These are manifestations of *responsibility, consequence, and center-of-mass*. They represent the weight that pulls you into orbit around something important.

**Thematic**: They're *grounding*. They pull you toward what matters. Bonding with them teaches you about *commitment* and *being held*.

**Psychological Mirror**: Gravity entities represent your need for meaning and connection. They're often heavy, slow, and reliable.

**Periliminal Correlation**: Gravity entities appear to players who are aimless or drifting. They offer purpose through weight.

---

### **Matter Entities: Form and Substance**

These are manifestations of *structure, body, and tangible reality*. They represent the *material* world and physical existence.

**Thematic**: They're *builders* and *makers*. They create lasting structures. Bonding with them teaches you about *creation* and *permanence*.

**Psychological Mirror**: Matter entities represent your capacity to build and create in the physical world. They're often complex in form and visually elaborate.

**Periliminal Correlation**: Matter entities appear to players struggling with *unreality* or dissociation. They anchor you back to the material.

---

### **Psyche Entities: Consciousness and Pattern**

These are manifestations of *mind, thought, and symbolic meaning*. They represent the *internal* landscape of consciousness.

**Thematic**: They're *reflective*. They show you patterns you might otherwise miss. Bonding with them teaches you about *self-knowledge* and *pattern recognition*.

**Psychological Mirror**: Psyche entities represent your capacity for introspection and understanding. They're often surreal in appearance and *deeply* unique to each player.

**Periliminal Correlation**: Psyche entities appear to curious/analytical players. They validate your search for meaning.

---

### **Quantum Entities: Possibility and Superposition**

These are manifestations of *potential, uncertainty, and multiple states*. They represent *unresolved possibility*.

**Thematic**: They're *transformative*. They exist in multiple states. Bonding with them teaches you about *potential* and *transformation*.

**Psychological Mirror**: Quantum entities represent your unrealized selves—the paths you didn't take, the versions of you that might still be possible.

**Periliminal Correlation**: Quantum entities appear to players at crossroads or facing major decisions. They embody the "roads not taken."

---

## Entity Evolution: Representing Growth

Each entity has three evolution stages, representing *maturation* and *specialization*.

**Stage 1**: The *proto* form—raw potential, unrefined. This is a young entity, learning.

**Stage 2**: The *developed* form—matured, specialized. This entity has chosen a *direction* and developed along that axis.

**Stage 3**: The *apex* form—fully realized. This is an entity at the peak of its category, rare, powerful.

Evolution is *not automatic*. You *choose* how to evolve an entity based on how you use it:

- Use an Energy entity in offensive battles? It evolves toward combat intensity.
- Use it defensively or for social purposes? It evolves toward power-of-presence.
- Leave it unused? It might devolve (a rare consequence) or *refuse* to evolve until you commit to a direction.

This teaches players that *relationship* with entities is mutual. They're not just tools; they're *responding* to your choices and your uses of them.

## Breeding: Creating New Meanings

When two entities breed (via the `CompanionBreeding` system), the offspring is a *symbolic combination* of the parents.

**Mechanical**: Offspring is always Stage 1. It inherits the higher-level parent's base category (with a chance for cross-category "mutation"). It gains abilities from both parents but starts weak.

**Narrative**: The breeding is a *combination of concepts*. An Energy + Gravity entity produces something that's about *directed force* (power with purpose). An Entropy + Psyche entity might produce something about *dissolution of self* or *loss of identity*.

**Thematic**: Breeding an entity is a *commitment act*. You're creating a new possibility that didn't exist before. It's generative and hopeful, even though it requires sacrifice (the parents are locked during gestation).

## Companion Roster: The 450 Playable Allies

The companion roster (distinct from entities) is designed with the *reverse* of entities:

- **Entities** are what you catch/discover and learn from.
- **Companions** are what you *build* or *select at creation* and *bond* with.

Each companion has:
- A **race** (20 options, tied to perception)
- A **frame** (20 options, tied to senses)
- A **mod** (20 options, tied to interaction method)
- A **faction** (4 options)
- A **rarity** (Common to Legendary)

This creates immense variety *before* breeding even enters. A player's companion isn't just a functional ally; it's a *statement of identity*.

**Example Companion Arcs**:

- **Prismatic + Zephyr + Sync + Factionless**: A light-seeing, free-spirited, synchronized companion. This player values clarity and freedom. Their companion is probably named something like "Clarity" or "Exodus."

- **Null + Behemoth + Titanium + Sovereign Crown**: A colorless, massive, reinforced companion. This player values stability and order. Their companion might be named "Structure" or "Law."

- **Abyssal + Flux + Psychic + Veiled Current**: A pressure-resilient, contradictory, mind-reading companion. This player values depth and mystery. Their companion might be named "Paradox" or "Echo."

## The Six-Layer Metaphor: Matryoshka Consciousness

The six layers can be understood as *layers of consciousness*:

- **Subliminal** = Autopilot consciousness (you're not fully awake)
- **Liminal** = Dream state (logic doesn't apply)
- **Supraliminal** = Waking consciousness (aware but constrained by social/systemic rules)
- **Hyperliminal** = Altered consciousness (intoxicated, euphoric, dissociated)
- **Extraliminal** = Collective consciousness (feeling others, group dynamics)
- **Periliminal** = Unconscious (the repressed material, the shadow, the fears you can't face)

Progression through the layers is *literal psychological progression*. Reaching Periliminal and achieving blessing isn't a game achievement; it's *integration of the unconscious*. You're making peace with your shadow.

## Emergent Narrative: Player-Created Stories

Because NPCs track your choices, entities have individual personality quirks, and factions have conflicting goals, the game *generates* narratives unique to each playthrough:

- Player A sides with Wildlands, catches a Quantum entity, and chooses a Mysterious companion. Their story is about embracing change and possibility. They're a different person by end-game.

- Player B sides with Sovereign Crown, methodically catches every Energy entity, and chooses a Regimented companion. Their story is about *perfection* through optimization and control. They're powerful but hollow.

- Player C stays Factionless, refuses to catch entities, and focuses on NPC relationships. Their story is about *connection* without domination. They're the character who changes the world through witness and testimony.

No two playthroughs are the same. The lore is *realized* through player choice.
