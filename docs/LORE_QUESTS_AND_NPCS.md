# Periliminal.Space: Quests, NPCs & Faction Arcs

## NPC Archetypes: Recurring Personalities Across Layers

Each layer has recurring NPC archetypes—versions of the same *role* with different degradations or enhancements based on the layer. A player might encounter "The Barista" in all six layers; in Subliminal, they're semi-present and tired. In Supraliminal, they're hyper-efficient. In Liminal, they're stuck in a loop, making the same drink forever. In Periliminal, they're twisted into something that serves psychological coffee.

### **The Barista** (Service Worker Archetype)

**Subliminal**: A tired coffee-shop employee who seems vaguely aware that they're going through the motions. They remember you from previous visits but can't quite place you. Small talk is difficult. They give hints about "something wrong" with the routine.

**Liminal**: Stuck behind an infinite coffee counter, making the same drink over and over. Each time you arrive, they're at the exact same point in the sequence—steam rises, cup fills, they turn to hand it to you. If you interrupt the loop, they don't understand what's happening.

**Supraliminal**: Hyper-competent, predictive barista. They know your order before you say it. They work with mechanical precision. They're *aware* that everything is optimized but don't seem bothered. They drop hints that the Sovereign Crown monitors "consistency metrics" for all service workers.

**Hyperliminal**: Works at the Catsino's 24-hour coffee bar. They never sleep. They remember *everyone* and every order ever taken. They're addicted to their job and to the gambling that the casino allows them between shifts. They can be bribed for information.

**Extraliminal**: Depends on faction control. In Sovereign territory, they're uniformed and formal. In Wildlands territory, they're rough and suspicious. In Veiled Current territory, they speak in metaphor about "brewing the perfect blend of fate."

**Periliminal**: Serves the coffee of *your specific addiction*. In your gauntlet, the barista offers what you're psychologically craving—comfort, stimulation, meaning. Each sip pulls you deeper into complacency or desperation, depending on your Hope profile's vulnerabilities.

**Quest Hooks**:
1. **"The Perfect Drink"** (Factionless): The Subliminal barista hints that they *remember* you from before the layers existed. Find proof that this NPC was different once.
2. **"Break the Loop"** (Veiled Current): Free the Liminal barista from their cycle. Requires understanding the ritual that binds them.
3. **"Consistency Audit"** (Sovereign Crown): The Supraliminal barista is scheduled for "optimization review." Do you help them pass or expose the audit as a cover for something darker?
4. **"The House Edge"** (Wildlands Ascendant): The Hyperliminal barista is drowning in debt. Help them cheat the casino or let them burn?
5. **"Addiction Spiral"** (Periliminal challenge): Resist the Periliminal barista's offers and uncover what *they* are addicted to.

### **The Archivist** (Knowledge-Keeper Archetype)

**Subliminal**: A reclusive librarian or blogger who collects "oddities"—stories about Liminal experiences, glitchy Subliminal events. They're considered a conspiracy nut, but their evidence is meticulous. They move slowly, speak carefully.

**Liminal**: Dwelling in a library that extends infinitely. They've cataloged every book, every shelf, but the library keeps growing. They're both helpful and trapped. They can point you to information but seem unable to leave.

**Supraliminal**: A corporate information specialist in a sleek office. They have *curated* knowledge—they know what the Sovereign Crown allows them to know. They're useful but censored. They leave gaps in their information that suggest hidden truths.

**Hyperliminal**: A casino historian who remembers every big win, every legendary player, every scandal. They're a storyteller, charismatic and charming. They know secrets that can be traded for casino chips.

**Extraliminal**: The archivists are *factionalized*. Each faction has its own keeper of records. They compete to control the narrative of what happened and what matters.

**Periliminal**: The archivist is *your personal memory keeper*. They recite back things you've forgotten, things you've suppressed. They know you better than you know yourself, and they weaponize that knowledge.

**Quest Hooks**:
1. **"The Unwritten History"** (Factionless): Before the layers, what was Dallas like? Find fragments in the Subliminal archivist's papers.
2. **"The Infinite Library"** (Veiled Current): The Liminal library is the *true* record of reality. Help the archivist catalog it, and they'll tell you the deepest truth.
3. **"Redacted Files"** (vs. Sovereign Crown): Uncover what the Sovereign Crown archivist is *not* allowed to tell you.
4. **"The House Historian"** (Hyperliminal): Play a high-stakes game for access to secrets the historian knows.
5. **"Memory Palace Invasion"** (Wildlands Ascendant): Raid the faction archivists' records to steal their intelligence.

### **The Authority** (Power-Holder Archetype)

**Subliminal**: A police officer, manager, or HOA representative. They're doing their job but seem *unusually* calm about the weird stuff happening. They might warn you off certain areas or suggest you "go home and forget about it."

**Liminal**: A figure in formal wear sitting at an impossible desk in an empty hallway. They have rules for the hallway (don't run, don't look back, don't ask questions). They don't enforce the rules; reality does.

**Supraliminal**: A corporate executive, government official, or other symbol of order. They're charming, competent, and *genuinely* believe their authoritarian systems are for everyone's good. They can't conceive of alternatives.

**Hyperliminal**: The casino's security chief or a high-ranking dealer. They maintain order through a mix of charm and threat. They're actually more flexible than the Supraliminal authority—they allow rule-bending if you're clever enough and pay the house.

**Extraliminal**: Faction representatives. Military commanders, cult leaders, gang captains. They're the ones ordering entity deployments and claiming territory.

**Periliminal**: A *parental figure*—someone whose approval you desperately needed and never got, or whose disapproval you fear. They judge you in your gauntlet, and their judgment is *crushing*.

**Quest Hooks**:
1. **"Question Authority"** (Factionless): The Subliminal authority knows more than they're saying. Follow them to find out what.
2. **"The Desk Keeper"** (Veiled Current): Negotiate with the Liminal authority to change the rules of their hallway.
3. **"Corporate Collapse"** (Wildlands Ascendant): Sabotage the Supraliminal authority's perfect system to prove it's fragile.
4. **"Security Clearance"** (Sovereign Crown): Help the Supraliminal or Hyperliminal authority maintain their order. Get promoted.
5. **"Parental Approval"** (Periliminal): Survive your gauntlet's authoritarian challenge without breaking psychologically.

### **The Lover** (Connection-Seeker Archetype)

**Subliminal**: Someone you're dating or want to date. They're kind but seem *distant*, like they're not fully present. They sometimes say things that suggest they remember a different version of you or a different timeline.

**Liminal**: A ghost or memory of past love. They're beautiful but fading. Interacting with them becomes increasingly painful because you know they're not real, but they *feel* real.

**Supraliminal**: A perfect partner—too perfect. Their personality is optimized for compatibility. They have no flaws, which means they have no authenticity. The Sovereign Crown might be *engineering* this relationship.

**Hyperliminal**: A seductive, amoral lover who plays with you as they play the casino. They're thrilling and destructive. They might genuinely care, or they might just be playing you. You can't tell.

**Extraliminal**: A factional representative sent to seduce you and turn you toward their side. Or a genuine NPC who happens to be aligned with a faction, creating relationship tension.

**Periliminal**: Your deepest insecurity about intimacy. The gauntlet shows you a version of your lover who *leaves you*, or *uses you*, or *doesn't actually know you*.

**Quest Hooks**:
1. **"Lost in Translation"** (Factionless): Your Subliminal lover remembers something about before the layers. Reconnect with them and learn what.
2. **"Let Them Go"** (Veiled Current): Help the Liminal lover's ghost find peace and move on.
3. **"Authenticity"** (Wildlands Ascendant): Prove to your Supraliminal lover that they have real wants. Break their programming.
4. **"House of Love"** (Hyperliminal): Make a binding bet with your Hyperliminal lover about the nature of your relationship.
5. **"Faction Pressure"** (Vs. Factions): Your lover is being pressured to recruit you. Do you leave them, join their faction, or sabotage their recruitment?

---

### **The Reflection** (Self-Archetype)

**Subliminal**: You occasionally glimpse yourself acting *differently* than you intended. A reflection that doesn't match your actions. A moment of missing time.

**Liminal**: A literal mirror-self, trapped in mirrors and reflective surfaces. They're you, but making different choices. They might be hostile or begging for help. Interacting with them risks *swapping* places.

**Supraliminal**: A version of you optimized by the Sovereign Crown. They have your talents but none of your doubts. They're wearing your life. Meeting them is *disturbing*.

**Hyperliminal**: A version of you who won big at the casino. They're everything you could be if you had better luck. Or worse luck and worse choices. Either way, they're a mirror of potential.

**Extraliminal**: Your factional counterpart. If you're Factionless, you see factional versions of yourself. If you're Sovereign Crown, you see wild Wildlands versions.

**Periliminal**: The version of you that *breaks*. Your gauntlet forces you to confront your worst self and decide whether to accept, fight, or integrate them.

**Quest Hooks**:
1. **"Who Am I?"** (Factionless): Investigate your Subliminal reflection. Are you losing time? Is someone pretending to be you?
2. **"Mirror Swap"** (Veiled Current): Enter the Liminal mirror and understand why your reflection made different choices.
3. **"Optimization Resistance"** (vs. Sovereign Crown): Stop the Supraliminal version of you from replacing you.
4. **"Lucky Break"** (Hyperliminal): Play against your Hyperliminal self for ultimate stakes.
5. **"Integration"** (Periliminal): Accept your broken self and become whole, or shatter completely.

## Faction Quest Lines: Deep Story Arcs

Each faction offers a *complete narrative arc* that reveals faction secrets, tests player loyalty, and fundamentally changes how the player understands the world.

### **Sovereign Crown: The Optimization Path**

**Act 1: Welcome to Structure**
- Location: Supraliminal's gleaming corporate hub
- Quest 1: "Consistency Metrics" — Audit three NPCs' daily routines. Confirm they're repeating identical actions.
- Quest 2: "The Deviation" — One of the audited NPCs has broken their routine. Report it or help them hide.
- Quest 3: "Integration Review" — Attend a briefing where the Crown explains that "consistency deviations" are being addressed.
- **Revelation**: The Crown isn't just monitoring NPCs—it's *enforcing* their routines. Free will is optional.

**Act 2: Ascension Through Measurement**
- Location: The Crown's "Perfection Laboratory" in Supraliminal
- Quest 4: "Efficiency Trials" — Compete in optimized combat scenarios. The more efficient your tactics, the higher your score.
- Quest 5: "The Ascension Frame" — You're offered a second frame at Champion level. The Crown claims it's a "natural progression." It's a *control mechanism*.
- Quest 6: "Double Vision" — Your ascended frame reports your actions to the Crown. Knowingly or unconsciously?
- **Revelation**: Champions aren't elevated; they're *co-opted*. The Crown uses them to police other players.

**Act 3: The Perfect World**
- Location: Supraliminal's innermost sanctum
- Quest 7: "The Algorithm" — Meet Magistra Kross. She shows you the algorithm that "optimizes" the world. It's terrifyingly consistent.
- Quest 8: "Deviation Protocols" — Execute protocols on NPCs who are "breaking optimization." This is morally horrific.
- Quest 9: "The Final Consistency" — Kross offers you the "final upgrade": full integration with the Sovereign Crown's network. You'd be perfectly optimized. You'd also lose individual agency.
- **Climax Choices**:
  - **Accept**: Become a Crown agent. Gain incredible power. Lose yourself.
  - **Reject**: Actively sabotage Crown infrastructure. Become a fugitive. Gain powerful allies among the Factionless.
  - **Subvert**: Pretend to accept while secretly working to break the algorithm from within.

**Rewards**:
- Crown Champions get faction-exclusive entities (higher starting stats, faction-bonus abilities).
- Saboteurs get faction-exclusive infiltration tech (undetectability, surveillance bypass).
- Subversives get *both*, plus access to Crown secrets that can be traded to other factions.

---

### **Veiled Current: The Surrender Path**

**Act 1: The Calling**
- Location: Progressively deeper into Liminal's non-Euclidean zones
- Quest 1: "The First Vision" — Encounter a Veiled Current recruiter who speaks in riddles. Solve riddle, gain entry to their circles.
- Quest 2: "Ritual of Listening" — Perform a meditation in Liminal. The world speaks to you in visual metaphor. Interpret it correctly, or the ritual traps you.
- Quest 3: "Gift from the Current" — You're given a *current-stone*, a crystallized piece of Liminal logic. It's beautiful and slightly corrupting.
- **Revelation**: The Veiled Current isn't organized by hierarchy; it's organized by *attunement*. Those who've surrendered most completely rise highest.

**Act 2: Deeper Surrender**
- Location: The Heart of Liminal (procedurally generated, unique to each player)
- Quest 4: "Sacrifice" — To go deeper, you must surrender something precious. A memory, a relationship, a belief. The ritual is real; the sacrifice is permanent.
- Quest 5: "The Voices Multiply" — Your connection to Liminal deepens. You start hearing *many* voices. Are they the Veiled Current, or are they hallucinations?
- Quest 6: "The Veiled Heart Speaks" — You're granted an audience with the collective consciousness. They show you the "truth" of the layers. It's *terrifying* and *beautiful*.
- **Revelation**: The layers are alive. They're not accidents; they're *aware*. The Veiled Current serves them willingly.

**Act 3: Assimilation**
- Location: The deepest Liminal, where non-Euclidean geometry breaks down into pure abstraction
- Quest 7: "The Final Surrender" — Become one with Liminal. Your body transforms. You're no longer fully human.
- Quest 8: "Prophecy Weaving" — You learn to channel Liminal's visions and distribute them to other players. You become a Voice.
- Quest 9: "The Transformation Complete" — You return to the other layers, but you're *changed*. NPCs react differently to you. You're unsettling and insightful.
- **Climax Choices**:
  - **Full Surrender**: Complete transformation. Become a Veiled Heart avatar. Gain reality-warping powers. Lose individuality.
  - **Balanced Surrender**: Keep your self but fully embrace serving the Veiled Current. Gain prophetic abilities. Remain partly yourself.
  - **Reject**: Break free from the indoctrination. The Veiled Current tries to *pull* you back. Become a fugitive with powerful psychic scars.

**Rewards**:
- Full Surrenderers get faction-exclusive Liminal-native entities (phase shift, non-Euclidean movement, prophecy abilities).
- Balanced Players get prophecy and divination bonuses, plus the ability to navigate Liminal without getting lost.
- Rejecters get intel on the Veiled Current's true nature and can trade that to other factions for significant rewards.

---

### **Wildlands Ascendant: The Primal Path**

**Act 1: Rejection of Order**
- Location: Liminal's forests and Subliminal's abandoned green spaces
- Quest 1: "The Fittest" — Prove your physical capability in a trials gauntlet. Out-run, out-climb, out-fight the Spore Prophet's scouts.
- Quest 2: "Natural Selection" — You're made aware of "weak" players who are being culled. Do you help them or let natural selection take its course?
- Quest 3: "Adaptation" — Exposure to Wildlands entities begins to *change* you. Your body adapts. It's not painful; it's *exhilarating*.
- **Revelation**: Wildlands Ascendant isn't just anarchists; they're *evolutionary*. They believe humanity is the cocoon; something greater is emerging.

**Act 2: Primal Transformation**
- Location: The Spore Prophet's territory in deep Liminal forests
- Quest 4: "The First Blooming" — The Spore Prophet exposes you to their condition. You begin to sprout fungal growths. You gain new abilities; you also gain *disturbing* new instincts.
- Quest 5: "The Hive Nexus" — You're introduced to other Ascendants who've transformed. They're not unified by communication; they're unified by *pheromones* and collective instinct.
- Quest 6: "Apotheosis or Death" — The final stage: complete transformation or rejection and potentially death.
- **Revelation**: The Spore Prophet isn't human anymore. They're the vector for a *rewiring* of consciousness—not alien, but post-human.

**Act 3: The New World**
- Location: Everywhere (as a transformed Ascendant, you're visible across all layers)
- Quest 7: "Recruitment" — You're sent to "infect" other players with the Spore Prophet's influence. You can do this through combat, through seduction, through philosophy.
- Quest 8: "Pressure from Within" — The transformation is *still ongoing*. You're changing in unexpected ways. Are you becoming part of the Spore Prophet's hive, or is your individual will reasserting itself?
- Quest 9: "The Final Evolution" — Face the Spore Prophet. Are they truly a prophet, or just a vector? Is the evolution good or parasitic?
- **Climax Choices**:
  - **Full Transformation**: Become one of the evolved. Gain symbiotic abilities (better entities through infectious spreading). Lose human form (cosmetic changes, unable to hide).
  - **Balanced Evolution**: Keep your form but enhanced. Gain natural bonuses. Remain partially isolated from Ascendant hive-mind.
  - **Reject**: Fight back against the transformation. Survive with scars (cosmetic mutation). Gain immunity to Ascendant recruitment.

**Rewards**:
- Full Evolutionaries get faction-exclusive entities (infectious, spreading, symbiotic).
- Balanced Players get natural stat bonuses (strength, speed, adaptation).
- Rejecters get anti-Ascendant tech and can help other players resist the Spore Prophet.

### **Factionless: The Witness Path**

**Act 1: Survival and Observation**
- Location: The forgotten spaces—Subliminal's empty houses, Liminal's dead ends, Supraliminal's maintenance tunnels
- Quest 1: "The Wanderer's Guild" — Meet other Factionless players. Exchange intel. Survive without factional protection.
- Quest 2: "The Three Doors" — Encounter evidence that all three factions are *wrong* about something fundamental. Gather that evidence.
- Quest 3: "The Oracle's Riddle" — A mysterious figure (the Oracle) speaks to you in unsolvable paradoxes. Each riddle hints at the truth beneath the layers.
- **Revelation**: The factions aren't natural; they're *constructed*. Someone or something *created* them to control the narrative.

**Act 2: Investigation and Convergence**
- Location: Moving between faction territories, gathering secrets
- Quest 4: "Crown Secrets" — Steal or barter for a Crown secret: the optimization algorithm's true purpose.
- Quest 5: "Current Truths" — Infiltrate a Veiled Current ritual and record what they're actually channeling.
- Quest 6: "Ascendant Origins" — Find evidence of what the Spore Prophet was before their transformation.
- **Revelation**: The factions are fragments of a *larger thing* that shattered. Each faction is one piece, mutated and adapted.

**Act 3: The Witness Becomes the Sage**
- Location: The deepest Factionless territories (places that shouldn't exist, between the layers)
- Quest 7: "The Archive" — The Factionless maintain a hidden archive of *true* history. Add your findings.
- Quest 8: "The Thief's Gambit" — The Thief (a Factionless legend) teaches you how to steal from factions without them knowing.
- Quest 9: "The Singularity Point" — You're offered a choice: remain neutral and keep witnessing, or *act* on what you know.
- **Climax Choices**:
  - **Perfect Neutrality**: Become the Oracle's successor. You see all, know all, say nothing. Gain omniscient perspective (can see through all faction illusions). Become isolated.
  - **Balanced Witness**: Gather allies among the Factionless and Factionless-aligned players. Use your knowledge to *negotiate*. Remain yourself.
  - **Active Rebellion**: Expose all three factions' secrets publicly. Become a target but gain powerful fugitive allies.

**Rewards**:
- Oracle successors get reality-perception bonuses (see through illusions, understand the true nature of things).
- Balanced Witnesses get diplomatic bonuses and can broker deals between hostile factions.
- Rebels get access to all faction secrets and can trade them for incredible rewards from other Factionless players.

## NPC Relationship Flags

NPCs track your choices. Certain quests alter NPC disposition, which affects future quests and dialogue. For example:

- **The Barista**: If you freed them from Liminal loops, they're grateful and trustworthy. If you exploited them for info, they're wary. If you recruited them for a faction, they're conflicted.
- **The Archivist**: If you helped them catalog Liminal's library, they'll share secrets. If you stole knowledge, they'll sabotage your other quests. If you exposed them to the Sovereign Crown, they're hunted, and you can either rescue them or turn them in.
- **The Authority**: If you obeyed them, you're their asset. If you defied them, they'll actively work against you. If you compromised with them, they're unpredictable allies.

The system is designed so that *no* playthrough is the same. Your choices create a web of obligations, enmities, and friendships that cascade through the game.

## Quest Reward: Titles

High-tier quests grant titles (not cosmetics, actual titles in your signature). Examples:

- "The Unbound" (reject all factions)
- "The Synchronized" (reach maximum Sovereign Crown progression)
- "The Surrendered" (reach maximum Veiled Current progression)
- "The Evolved" (reach maximum Wildlands progression)
- "The Witness" (complete all Factionless questlines)
- "The Breaker of Chains" (sabotage all three major factions)
- "The Pariah" (betray every NPC you meet)
- "The Beloved" (maintain maximum relationship with 5+ NPCs)

Each title grants identity seed multiplier and changes how NPCs respond to you. Titles can also *conflict*—you can't be "The Synchronized" and "The Unbound" simultaneously.
